0-Cribbage does not resemble Cribbage at all (as you might expect), although it is cute.

The throwing and pegging phases of each hand now finish instantly, leaving only the turn-up and counting phases.

This means there are only two ways to earn points in 0-Cribbage:

  • A one-card flush (1 point)
  • His heels (2 points) for cutting up a Jack

This means the dealer will automatically receive 2 points per hand, while a non-dealer will get 1. In addition to that, the dealer has a 13 in 52 chance of getting two more points, for a weighted average of 2.154 points per hand for the dealer. This gives whoever wins the deal a sizeable advantage.

Essentially, 0-Cribbage boils down to a drawn-out coin flip with a small chance of an upset, assuming there is no strategy involved in cutting the deck (which there isn’t unless cheating is involved).
